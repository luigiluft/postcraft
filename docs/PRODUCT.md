# Product & GTM

> Distilled from `docs/RESEARCH.md` (7-agent foundation research). Numbers are competitor reference points, not commitments.

## What it is

A **Portuguese-native B2B content engine** that turns a company's public footprint into ICP-targeted carousels & images — where the **design craft and ICP targeting**, not the ingestion, are the headline. The loop, not a feature.

## Why now / the gap

The market splits into four camps, none owning the full loop:

| Camp | Examples | Owns | Misses |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ad-creative engines | AdCreative.ai ($39–599) | competitor intel, creative scoring | templated output, no carousel craft |
| Social generators | Predis.ai ($19–212), Ocoya, SocialPost.ai | carousels + brand kit, website→content | shallow visuals, vertical-not-ICP targeting |
| Carousel specialists | PostNitro ($10), ContentDrips | layout discipline | no intelligence layer |
| Writing suites | Jasper ($39), Copy.ai ($49) | brand voice from site | visuals an afterthought |

Two axes are weak across the **whole field**: real ICP targeting and per-slide carousel craft. BR players (GalilAI, Postig) are thin template tools with no ICP or auto-ingestion.

## The 3 wedges we own

1. **Company-intelligence → editable ICP graph as the engine.** A steerable ICP (pains, verbatim vocabulary, objections, triggers, proof) re-targets every post — not "pick an audience".
2. **Per-slide carousel art direction.** Each slide has its own anatomy; the opposite of one template stamped 6×.
3. **Competitor/reference visual research fused into generation.** Rivals' + global best-in-class visual language becomes design tokens that art-direct output — shown alongside the result for trust.

## Who buys (wedge)

**B2B agencies and B2B social/content marketers** — highest willingness to pay, run many brands, feel the carousel-craft pain acutely. Beachhead: **BR B2B commerce/distribution** (the Agrega vertical) where a Portuguese-first, ICP-aware tool has a real local opening before global players localize depth.

## Pricing & packaging (recommendation)

- **Per-brand workspace + usage credits**, anchored against AdCreative ($39 / $249), NOT $19 schedulers.
  - *Starter* — 1 brand, N content kits/mo.
  - *Studio* — few brands, more credits, spec editor + scheduling.
  - *Agency* — many brands, seats, approval flow, white-label export.
- **Activation "aha":** paste a domain → a finished, on-brand carousel in < 2 minutes. Gate *volume and export/white-label*, not the first taste.
- **BR:** bill in BRL via **Pix** (incl. Pix Automático for subscriptions).

## 0→1 motion

1. Dogfood on Agrega + 3–5 friendly BR B2B brands; publish the carousels, collect proof.
2. Wedge into agencies with a "1 brand free kit" demo (the engine already runs zero-key for the sample).
3. Charge at the agency tier first (clearest ROI), expand down to in-house marketers.

## Top risks → design answers

1. **Auto-ingestion isn't novel** → moat is the *loop* + carousel craft, not the crawl.
2. **AI text-in-image looks broken** → hybrid renderer keeps text deterministic.
3. **Generic "AI slop" look** → the visual playbook + anti-template `dontList` enforced in prompts & grammar.
4. **Hallucinated stats** → proof provenance classes; no number ships without a real proof asset.
5. **Scraper/social drift & cost** → adapter seam + pilot gates + content-hash cache.
6. **Voice mismatch** → voice profile derived from the brand's own high-engagement posts.
7. **Provider lock-in / margin** → everything behind adapters; model routing for cost.

## MVP to charge money (v1)

Domain in → harvested intelligence + **editable ICP** → 1 reviewed carousel (legible, on-brand, per-slide art) → export. One brand, one language (pt-BR), credits-metered. Everything else is expansion.
